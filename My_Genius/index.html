Nuclear Plants make up 10% of the energy we use as of right now.
Many of them a built before the 2000s (1970 - 1990) and often use a reactor called light water.
There are many different types of reactors but this one is cheap and easy to make.
Which is why it is so popular.
If you may not know, nuclear reactors produce energy by nuclear fission.


What happens here is that the nucleus of an atom is split into pieces, and during this process, there is a BANG
Which, generates a lot of energy and radiation.
They use this energy to heat water and create electricity.
Uranium is used to create these bangs and their fuel lasts about 8 years.
After their life span runs out, they are still emitting radiation.
Luckily water is good at supressing the radiation. But with radiation is heat.
To make sure that the water doesn't boil out, there is power that is constantly cooling the water.
Without that power, the water will boil and cause a huge BANG in the building.
