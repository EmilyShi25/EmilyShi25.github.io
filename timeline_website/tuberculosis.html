1900: Tuberculosis

"The Trailing Skirt: Death Loves a Shining Mark" From the Pluck Magazine in 1900 depicted a women with dust flying from her long trailing skirt. During this time period, doctors related long skirts and the germs that they picked up with tuberculosis. Which, surprisingly was fetishized for the pale complexion that it gave its victims (Mullin). Between late 18th century and mid 19th century (1780 - 1850), tuberculosis was deemed as attractive because of the current beauty standards for paper-pale skin, rosy cheeks and thin body. All of which were given to those who suffer the disease. Showing just how extreme people would go to get the perfect pale complexion. Not only does this shape health but also further justify the views on slavery and colonialism during the time. After all, white skin was considered superior (Colonialism)
