Nuclear Waste and What it is


Nuclear Plants make up 10% of the energy we use in the world as of right now
And are considered the second cleanest energy source after wind mills.
Many of them a built before the 2000s (1970 - 1990) and often use a reactor called light water.
There are many different types of reactors but this one is cheap and easy to make.
Which is why it is so popular. But nuclear plants results in something that coupd potentially be extremely toxic.
If you may not know, nuclear reactors produce energy by nuclear fission.


What happens here is that the nucleus of an atom is split into pieces, this process creates a huge BANG.
Which, generates a lot of energy.
They use this energy to heat water and create electricity.
Uranium is used to create these bangs and their fuel lasts about 8 years.
After their life span runs out, they are still able to emit energy, in the form of radiation.
The uranium waste is then usually put into pools that resides within the nuclear power plants.
Water is good at supressing radiation and uses the energy from the nuclear reactor to cool the water.
This is because like stated earlier, the uranium can still omitt energy, or you can call it, heat.
If the temperature gets too high and the water boils out, high radiation will be released
